Appetizer

Appetizers in the Filipino cuisine are also known as “hand food”, called pulutan. The pulutan are served most often with liquors and non-alcoholic beverages, and will be offered in restaurants as well as local dinners, not matter the country’s region.

Main Dish

Filipino cuisine is influenced by other cultures. One of the main features of Filipino cuisine is its diversity and complexity.

Dessert

The Philippines ingenuity with its desserts roots back to its history with sugar, which was a key product in its colonial economy. Filipinos use sugar in everything, which is why it’s not surprising that they have a wide variety of sweet delicacies.
